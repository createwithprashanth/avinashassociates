Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before reaching out

A Private Limited Company typically takes 7–10 working days after all documents are submitted. An LLP takes 5–7 days. OPC is usually 5–7 days as well. Delays can occur if the chosen company name is rejected by MCA or documents need corrections.

You'll need: PAN card of the business/owner, Aadhaar card, proof of business address (electricity bill / rent agreement), bank account details, and photos. For a company, additionally the Certificate of Incorporation and MOA/AOA are required.

For individuals (non-audit): 31st July of the assessment year. For businesses requiring audit: 31st October. For companies: 31st October. Late filing attracts a penalty of ₹5,000 (₹1,000 if income < ₹5 lakh) plus interest under Section 234A.

Most regular taxpayers file GSTR-1 monthly (outward supplies) and GSTR-3B monthly (summary return + payment). Small businesses with turnover < ₹5 crore can opt for quarterly filing under the QRMP scheme. An annual return (GSTR-9) is due by 31st December.

The MCA levies a late fee of ₹100 per day per form with no upper cap. Missing annual filings (AOC-4, MGT-7) for multiple years can result in the company being struck off the register, making directors disqualified. Early filing is always strongly recommended.

Absolutely. We handle all NRI tax filings 100% remotely via email and video calls. We assist with ITR filing, DTAA benefits, FEMA compliance, 15CA/CB certificates for remittances, and NRE/NRO account advisory — all without you needing to travel to India.

Pvt Ltd is ideal if you plan to raise VC funding, have multiple shareholders, or want to issue ESOPs. LLP suits service-based businesses, professional firms, or those wanting lower compliance burden. LLPs cannot raise equity funding. We offer a free consultation to help you decide.

Yes — all our services are available online. Documents are collected via secure upload, consultations happen on video call, and deliverables are sent digitally. Clients across India and 6+ countries manage their Indian compliance with us entirely online.

Form 15CA is a self-declaration filed online by the remitter before sending money abroad. Form 15CB is a certificate from a CA confirming that applicable taxes have been paid or are not deductible. Both are mandatory for most foreign remittances above ₹5 lakh per financial year.
